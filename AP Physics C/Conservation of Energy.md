# Conservation of Energy

**Conservation of Energy** is one of the fundamental conservation laws in mechanics. In AP Physics C, it needs to be applied to systems that include **rotation**.

## Statement

The **total energy** of an isolated system remains constant:

$$E_{total} = \text{constant}$$

or

$$\Delta E_{total} = 0$$

When only **conservative forces** do work, **mechanical energy** is conserved:

$$K + U = \text{constant}$$

## Mechanical Energy with Rotation

For rotating systems, total mechanical energy includes **rotational kinetic energy**:

$$E = K_{trans} + K_{rot} + U$$

- $K_{trans} = \frac{1}{2}mv_{CM}^2$ **translational kinetic energy** of the center of mass
- $K_{rot} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$ **rotational kinetic energy**
- $U$ **potential energy** (gravitational, elastic, gravitational potential, etc.)

## Application in Celestial Mechanics

In satellite orbital transfer, conservation of mechanical energy applies:

$$E_1 = E_2$$
$$-\frac{GMm}{2r_1} = -\frac{GMm}{2r_2} + W_{\text{engine}}$$

This can be used to calculate the energy required for orbital maneuvering.
