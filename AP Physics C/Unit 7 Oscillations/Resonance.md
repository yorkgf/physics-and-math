# Resonance

**Resonance** is a special phenomenon in **driven oscillations**. When the driving frequency equals the **natural frequency** of the system, the amplitude becomes maximum.

## Natural Frequency

The natural frequency of an undamped system (for a mass-spring system):

$$\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

## Resonance Condition

When the driving frequency satisfies $\omega_{\text{drive}} = \omega_0$, **resonance** occurs and the amplitude reaches its maximum value.

## Resonance Curve

- The height and width of the resonance peak depend on the **damping coefficient**
- Smaller damping → sharper resonance peak
- Larger damping → broader, lower resonance peak

## Quality Factor

$$Q = \frac{\omega_0}{\Delta\omega}$$

Where $\Delta\omega$ is the **full width at half maximum** (FWHM) or half-power bandwidth. Larger $Q$ means sharper resonance.

## Example Problem

**Example:** An oscillating system has natural frequency $f_0 = 10$ Hz with very small damping, and quality factor $Q = 50$. Find the resonance frequency and the half-power bandwidth.

**Solution:**
Resonance frequency $\approx f_0 = 10$ Hz

Half-power bandwidth: $\Delta f = \frac{f_0}{Q} = \frac{10}{50} = 0.2$ Hz
