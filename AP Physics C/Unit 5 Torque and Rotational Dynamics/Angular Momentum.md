# Angular Momentum

**Angular momentum** is a core concept in AP Physics C. You need to master the formulas for both particles and rigid bodies.

## Angular Momentum of a Particle

$$\vec{L} = \vec{r} \times \vec{p} = \vec{r} \times m\vec{v}$$

Where $\vec{r}$ is the position vector from the reference point to the particle.

## Rigid Body Rotating About a Fixed Axis

$$L = I\omega$$

Where:
- $I$ = **moment of inertia** about the axis
- $\omega$ = **angular velocity**

## Angular Momentum Theorem

The **net external torque** equals the rate of change of angular momentum with respect to time:

$$\vec{\tau}_{\text{net}} = \frac{d\vec{L}}{dt}$$

## Example Problem

**Example:** A particle of mass $m = 2$ kg moves at speed $v = 3$ m/s in a circle of radius $r = 0.5$ m. Find the magnitude of its angular momentum.

**Solution:**
$$L = rmv = 0.5 \times 2 \times 3 = 3 \text{ kg·m}^2/\text{s}$$

The direction is perpendicular to the plane of the circle (determined by right-hand rule).
