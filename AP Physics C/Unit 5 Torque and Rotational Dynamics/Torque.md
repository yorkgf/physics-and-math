# Torque

**Torque** is the measure of the ability of a force to cause rotation about an axis. In AP Physics C, you need to understand the vector definition and calculations.

## Vector Form

$$\vec{\tau} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F}$$

The direction is given by the **right-hand rule** and is perpendicular to the plane containing $\vec{r}$ and $\vec{F}$.

## Scalar Form

$$\tau = rF\sin\theta = r_\perp F$$

Where $r_\perp = r\sin\theta$ is the **lever arm** (perpendicular distance from axis to line of action of force).

## Net Torque

$$\tau_{\text{net}} = \sum \tau_i$$

## Example Problem

**Example:** A force $F = 10$ N is applied at distance $r = 0.5$ m from the axis. The angle between the force and the position vector is $30^\circ$. Find the magnitude of the torque.

**Solution:**
$$\tau = rF\sin\theta = 0.5 \times 10 \times \sin 30^\circ = 5 \times 0.5 = 2.5 \text{ N·m}$$
