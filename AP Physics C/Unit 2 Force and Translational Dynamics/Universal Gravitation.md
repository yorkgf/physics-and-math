# Universal Gravitation

**Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation** is an important in-depth topic in AP Physics C. You need to master the derivation of gravitational potential energy and orbital mechanics.

## Law of Universal Gravitation

$$F = G\frac{m_1m_2}{r^2}$$

In vector form:
$$\vec{F}_{12} = -G\frac{m_1m_2}{r^2}\hat{r}_{12}$$

Where:
- $G$ = **gravitational constant** ($6.67 \times 10^{-11}$ N·m²/kg²)
- $m_1, m_2$ = masses of two objects
- $r$ = distance between centers of mass

## Gravitational Potential Energy

**Gravitational potential energy** (with zero potential energy at infinity):

$$U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$$

Derivation: $U = -\int_{\infty}^{r} \frac{GMm}{r'^2} \, dr' = -\frac{GMm}{r}$

## Gravitational Field

**Gravitational field strength** (force per unit mass):

$$\vec{g} = -\frac{GM}{r^2}\hat{r}$$

## Gravitational Force Inside a Uniform Sphere

For a uniform sphere of radius $R$ and density $\rho$, the gravitational force on a mass $m$ at distance $r$ from the center (where $r < R$) is:

$$F = -\frac{4\pi G\rho m}{3} \cdot r$$

This is a **linear restoring force** directed toward the center, similar to a spring force. An object dropped through a tunnel in such a planet would execute **simple harmonic motion**.

See: [[Example 7 - Ball in Tunnel Through Planet]]

## Escape Speed

The minimum speed required to escape from a planet's surface:

$$v_{\text{escape}} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$$

## Example Problem

**Example:** Earth has mass $M = 5.97 \times 10^{24}$ kg and radius $R = 6.37 \times 10^6$ m. Find the escape speed from Earth's surface.

**Solution:**
$$v_{\text{escape}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.97 \times 10^{24}}{6.37 \times 10^6}}$$
$$= \sqrt{1.25 \times 10^8} \approx 1.12 \times 10^4 \text{ m/s} = 11.2 \text{ km/s}$$
