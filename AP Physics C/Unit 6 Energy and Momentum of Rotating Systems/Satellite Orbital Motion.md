# Satellite Orbital Motion

**Satellite orbital motion** is a classic application where **gravitational force provides centripetal force**. AP Physics C requires you to master the derivation and analysis.

## Circular Orbital Speed

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

Derivation: $G\frac{Mm}{r^2} = m\frac{v^2}{r}$

## Orbital Period

$$T = 2\pi r\sqrt{\frac{r}{GM}} = \frac{2\pi r^{3/2}}{\sqrt{GM}}$$

## Orbital Energy

**Total mechanical energy** (for circular orbit):

$$E = K + U = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GMm}{r} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$

The negative sign indicates a **bound state**. Larger $r$ means higher total energy (less negative).

## Example Problem

**Example:** What is the altitude of a geosynchronous satellite? ($R_E = 6400$ km)

**Solution:**
Period of geosynchronous satellite $T = 24$ hours $= 86400$ s

From $T = 2\pi r^{3/2}/\sqrt{GM}$:
$$r^{3/2} = \frac{T\sqrt{GM}}{2\pi}$$

Substituting values: $r \approx 42164$ km (about $6.6 R_E$)

Altitude $h = r - R_E \approx 35764$ km
